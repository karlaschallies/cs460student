Augmented Reality Animal Cell Study Guide

This interactive model allows students to explore the internal structures of a eukaryotic animal cell. On mobile devices, the model can be viewed in augmented reality (AR). Click a hotspot to expand its description. Click anywhere else to collapse.

Structures Not Represented in This Model

This generalized animal cell model focuses on internal organelles. The following structures are specialized features found only in certain animal cell types and are therefore not shown.

Chromosomes

Organized structures of linear DNA associated with proteins (histones) that contain genetic information.

DNA

Molecule that contains the genetic blueprint of the organism, encoding the instructions for protein synthesis and cellular function.

Cytoskeleton

Network of protein fibers, including microfilaments and microtubules, that provides structure, shape, and support, and aids in intracellular transport and cell movement.

Cilia

Small but numerous external projections that beat in a coordinated pattern to create waves that move fluids such as mucus across the cell surface.

Flagella

Long, whip-like structure composed of microtubules arranged in a 9+2 pattern that provides motility to the cell.

Pseudopods

Temporary projections of the cytoplasm that allow the cell to move by extending toward a desired location, resembling “false feet.”
